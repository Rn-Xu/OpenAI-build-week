# Two-minute demo script

Record the actual product in landscape orientation. Keep the cursor visible, enlarge the browser to make text readable, and avoid showing API keys, local file paths, notifications, or private account information.

## 0:00–0:15 — The problem

**Visual:** Start on the Get up! landing screen. Keep the product name and input visible.

**Voice-over:**

> Most productivity tools are good at telling us what we need to do. But the hardest part is often not planning the task—it is starting. That is why we built Get up!

## 0:15–0:28 — The product

**Visual:** Place the cursor in the goal input.

**Voice-over:**

> Get up! is an AI action coach that turns an overwhelming goal into one small, immediate action. Instead of another long checklist, it answers one question: What is the easiest meaningful step you can take right now?

## 0:28–0:58 — Generate the first action

**Visual:** Enter: `I need to study for tomorrow's exam, but I feel overwhelmed.` Submit the goal and show the result.

**Voice-over:**

> I can describe my goal in ordinary language. Get up! responds with a concrete starting action: put my phone aside and open my notes. It is specific, achievable, and should take about one minute.

## 0:58–1:18 — Continue after completion

**Visual:** Click **Done**. Show the next action.

**Voice-over:**

> When I complete the action, Get up! gives me the next manageable step. I only need to focus on what comes next, not the entire task.

## 1:18–1:35 — Recover when stuck

**Visual:** Click **I'm stuck** or **Make it smaller**. Show the adapted action.

**Voice-over:**

> If a step still feels too difficult, I can ask for something smaller. The system adapts instead of judging me or forcing me to restart.

## 1:35–1:50 — How it was built

**Visual:** Show a brief architecture card or the application's About section; return quickly to the product.

**Voice-over:**

> We built Get up! with Python, Streamlit, and the OpenAI API. Codex helped us prototype the interface, refine the structured prompting workflow, and debug the experience during OpenAI Build Week.

## 1:50–2:05 — Close

**Visual:** Show a completed step and then the Get up! title card.

**Voice-over:**

> Get up! does not try to make people perfectly productive. It helps them do something more important: begin. Because motivation does not always create action. Sometimes action creates motivation.

## Recording notes

- Use real product behavior; do not edit the video to imply features that are not implemented.
- Target 1080p, 16:9, and 2:05 or shorter.
- Remove long loading pauses, but keep the input and response sequence understandable.
- Add English captions; use the voice-over text above as the caption source.
- Record one clean backup take before editing.
